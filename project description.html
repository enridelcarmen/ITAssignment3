About Food Helpers

What is Food Helpers?

The concept of the app is to either share items - people could have the possibility to donate items, maybe to different categories of people, like elderly people, people with disabilities, people in self isolation, health care workers, other frontline workers and recently unemployed people.

What is the state of the art of this new technology?

The app would be using existing technology or ideas that are currently being used in other current apps or websites. Some of the existing technology that would be used in the app is to firstly create an account. Once logged in users could search for items and then this would produce a scrollable list of items, either by text based listings or by image based listings. The individual listing could be selected, either by tapping or clicking to get further details of the item. A similar experience could be likened to “Mu Stuff, Your Stuff” on facebook or an ebay search. The difference with this is that items can be purely donated or traded with other users to get hard to find out of stock grocery items.

Another feature of this app is the ability to organise delivery of items, either to a safe drop zone or outside someone's home. This technology already exists and would be likened to uber eats. The delivery can be viewed live and an estimated time of arrival can be given so the user knows when the delivery has arrived at its destination. To further cut costs down for people who are already struggling, a delivery service could be provided free of charge with the condition that the delivery company has permission to take photos of deliveries to be able to post on their own social media pages for publicity and promotion. The customer would have to agree to this in the terms.

What technological or other developments make this possible?

As discussed above, there are technologies that currently exist that can be used to create the app. If using the facebook marketplace approach, there is currently the ability to create an account to join the service, which then enables you to communicate with other people, share stories and share photos. Marketplace has the features of payments which is another existing technology. Marketplace enables users to post items for sale. This type of technology can be used to create the food helpers app. Users can search for items, like items, save items for later or purchase items.

Other existing technologies which have been discussed above is the delivery driver technology which is used by companies such as uber and menulog. One area of technological advancements that may impact the area of delivery in the future is the idea of self-driving cars and drones eventually replacing delivery drivers. Amazon is testing out drones to carry out deliveries and this service is called Prime Air. This technology has the impact of changing the way items get delivered in the near future and this could change the way the app works with the currently proposed delivery driver system. This is something that may not happen straight away, but if companies are investing and testing the technology, it may not be too far away. The benefits are that there may be reduced costs in delivering items to a customer or user.

What is the likely impact?

The likely impact of the proposed app is that it will make grocery items available for people that are suffering hardship during the Covid-19 pandemic. When the Australian government enforced social distancing measures and encouraged people to stay at home, there were empty shelves in shops and many items became hard to source and/or acquire. The conceptual idea of the app came about from the idea of helping people who were not only struggling, self isolating, older or living with a disability to have access to grocery items that had become hard to find. When this idea or concept was first thought about, the world was in the early stages of the Coronavirus. In Australia, the rates of infection began to rise from mid to late March with a decline in rates of infection mid to late April and with a consistently smaller rate of infection over May.

What is likely to change? What is the potential impact of this development? Which people will be most affected and how?

With consistently low rates of infection there has been a very gradual easing of restrictions. Prior to this, panic buying was starting to ease and grocery items were being replenished in stores and customers were able to acquire hard to get items. In today's Coronavirus climate within Australia, the proposed app idea has lost some of its relevance. With this change, there is still a need to help people who are disadvantaged. People who are older, people living with disabilities, people who have been financially impacted and people who have been on “the front line”. This app has the ability to help people who fall into these categories.

Will this create, replace or make redundant any current jobs or technologies?

The app has the ability to help create jobs in the area of delivery driving either through a partnership with an existing company or gaining drivers for the app. If the app was successfully targeting people in need, then there would be a need for delivery drivers to deliver items.

How might this affect members of your family or your friends?

The app can affect family members or friends in that anyone who needs some form of assistance or support during hard times can use the app and source either free items or trade/bartered items or if people are in a position to be able to donate or trade items then they can do so through the app. The app may be able to help your family or friends if you cannot help them directly. If you are someone who is in need and can no longer provide for your family, this app can be another avenue to find some support or assistance in the form of obtaining grocery items. If you have family members or friends who have a disability, this can help people get groceries that are hard to get in times of crisis and get them delivered.
